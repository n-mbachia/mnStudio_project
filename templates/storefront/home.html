The Studio Method

From Forest
to Architecture

Every commission follows a rigorous, unhurried journey of selection, preservation, structural design, and traditional hand-joinery. We don't manufacture; we assemble structural history.

Scroll to explore craftsmanship
Phase I / Materialization

Timber Selection & Curing

Sustainably sourcing rare African hardwoods—including prime Mvule and seasoned Mahogany. Each slab undergoes a meticulous kiln-drying cycle and stress-grading to ensure centuries of dimensional stability.

Phase II / Formulation

Design & Blueprint Engineering

Translating timber geometry into balanced architectural blueprints. We draft custom frameworks and tailored joinery maps configured specifically to the temperature and spatial profile of your room.

Phase III / Creation

Traditional Hand Crafting

Executed completely via traditional mortise and tenon joinery. Zero metallic fasteners interfere with the structural expansion properties of the living wood, preserving integrity across generations.

Phase IV / Curation

White-Glove Installation

Delivered via specialized transport with meticulous structural assembly in situ. Your commission is complete only when positioned perfectly, sealed with custom organic oils, and handed over with lifelong documentation.

Commissions are
inherently limited.

To honor structural joinery standards and meticulous timber curing timelines, we cycle a strictly capped ledger of open commissions annually.
